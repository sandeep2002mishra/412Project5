Howdy! My name is Sandeep Mishra, and I am a junior Computer engineering student at Texas A&M. This website is meant to invoke my values as a computer science student and my journey as an undergrad so far. But in order to talk about my values, I would like to talk about my first failure in this joruney. As we all know, failure is the pillar of success and when I did my first software engineering project with a team, I learned a whole lot. The app is called H4U, which was done in my TACS club linked below this page, and was essentially supposed to be a fitness related app that went along with the health app(coming up in the next page!). The app tracks how many calories you lost or gained using a database API. Howevever, our team couldn't fullfill or manage the data generated as we were not consistent with normalizing data and optimizing and scaling indices. This resulted into poor user experience. Even though we didn't achieve what we wanted for this app, I learned a lot of great software engineering team lessons: planning, communicating, adapting.

Planning

This was argubly the most important step as a software engineer. The mistake my team made, was that we swifty planned our app development. What we learned is that if we had more meetings about what we needed to address and lay out the foundations for , we would have been better off at knowing the risks and responsibilities of using the database api that managed our project.

Communicating

As we became more frustated by the fact that our project was not to where we wanted, some of our teammates became less communicative. However, we understood in later projects too that even if we hit a wall, we should talk about the shortcomings and what we can do to address it. We later decided that for H4U, we would backtrack to where things worked and work on the health app instead. This was a major key in helping us at least have the right mindset for a better plan for another project.

Adapting

When we were working on H4U, there were many moments where we were managing scalability, but we were finding it quite challenging. In these moments, it is very hard to know whether to keep working on the issues or backtrack and find a different way to manage all the data. Looking back at the project, I could point to ways we at least attempted to adapt, whihc really helped when dealing with future projects that is in the techincal portfolio coming up!
